Assignment 1 - HISSS Game Visualization¶

We will be visualizing the states of the game HISSS in this assignment using matplotlib library. I will be visualizing the states of the game using two methods.

Before we begin, we will review the 50 cards available in the game. They are as follows :

  • 6 heads
  • 6 tails
  • 36 mid-sections
  • 2 wild cards

I will focus my visualization on one solution which leads to the longest snake with no repeated body cards. The length of that snake would be 8 cards and would consist of 8 rounds.

The visualization would show the cards that are valid for the player to pick according to the current state of the game. I am assuming that the player would pick only the cards that will lead to the desired snake. We will first show the visualization using the grid method.

1. Using grid method¶

The depiction is as follow:

  • Triangles with a black dot represent snake head.
  • Triangles without a black dot represent snake tail.
  • Squares with two colors represent snake body.
  • Multi-color triangle with a black dot represents wildcard head.
  • Multi-color triangle without a black dot represents wildcard tail.
  • We get yellow head snake.
  • We get yellow-orange body.
  • We get orange-red body.
  • We get red-purple body.
  • We get purple-blue body.
  • We get blue-green body.
  • We get green-yellow body.
  • We get yellow tail.

Now we will be simulating the same gameplay using the circular alignment method.

2. Using circular alignment method¶

The depiction is as follow:

  • Circles with a black dot represent snake head.
  • Circles without a black dot represent snake tail.
  • Circles with two colors represent snake body.
  • Multi-color circle with a black dot represents wildcard head.
  • Multi-color circle without a black dot represents wildcard tail.
  • We get yellow head snake.
  • We get yellow-orange body.
  • We get orange-red body.
  • We get red-purple body.
  • We get purple-blue body.
  • We get blue-green body.
  • We get green-yellow body.
  • We get yellow tail.

Both the visualization show invalid cards for the current state by blurring them and valid cards by not blurring them. The arrows shows the flow of gameplay till that round. The game ends when the player succeeds in creating a single snake.
